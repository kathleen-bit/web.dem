The Sun and The Stars


          1From a curious child who loved going outside to play with my friends to a teenager searching for her identity, and now a college student chasing her big dreams and big goals—I have come a long way. 2Each phase of my life has been a chapter in a book still being written and I would like to tell you, my story.



Childhood Years

          1 When I was a child, I used to have big dreams; becoming a president, a CEO of my own business, to become a billionaire and help people, become famous, and many other things. 2 I also remember that me and my siblings had this impossible recurring memory every time we got together—we said that we wanted to build a time machine. 3 How awesome would that be if we were able to do it? 4 I used to remember that I would be embarrassed when there was an assignment or project that asked— What is your childhood dream?, What is your dream when you were a child?, What did you want to do when you grow up? Thinking about it now, why was I so embarrassed to share it? Why was I so shy to share my big dreams? Is it because they would perceive me as arrogant and naïve? Sadly, yes. 5 Dreams are like stars that shine at night and a sun that brightens the day. 6 They say that as you grow older, those dreams will plummet and crash, and I wholeheartedly believed it. 7 I was in 5th grade when I first experienced that harsh reality. 8 I was young and naïve, yet I acted like I already knew the world. 9 Is it because I am the oldest? I think so, because as the eldest sibling, I was tasked with disciplining my younger brothers and sisters. 10 I would teach them what was right and wrong, tell them their responsibilities, and guide them.



          1 This makes me think at that time that I have so much knowledge and wisdom to share, and when things go wrong in our class ... maybe... just maybe I can be of any help; and boy, I was wrong! That day, we were doing an activity, the room was pitch silence, focusing on our own, and our teacher got out of the classroom and left us for a few minutes, and there’s this guy who is so loud – acting like he owns the place, shouting and laughing with his friends. 2 It gets to the point that we were not able to make our activity because we lost our interest in doing so. 3I remember that they were crumpling some papers and trying to shoot it into our small trash can like it was some kind of basketball. 3 The trash can got full, and thus, it overflowed. Our group of friends – mostly girls were so fed up with him that you could see it on our faces that we were annoyed by him. 4 So, me, being an ‘older sister’, came up to him and politely asked him to tone it down because we were doing an activity and we couldn’t focus when they were being loud and he just shunned me off and when you are a kid, you are prone to embarrassment and the need for acceptance by others. 5 Thus, I awkwardly tried it again, hoping that they would stop, but he just ignored me again. I felt humiliated and was put to shame. 6 At that time, I was so keen on being this wise and knowledgeable kid that even at school I followed through it. 7 Hence, I grabbed the collar of his shirt I was so pissed that I didn’t even say anything and just looked at him, piercingly through his eyes , Then he smirked; tried to punch me, but I evaded it; Then he tried to punch me again, and I avoided it again. 8 On the second punch, our classmates stopped us and separated us so that we could cool our heads off. At that time, I was so mad because I didn’t get to punch him, But thinking about it now, it’s okay because I know that he was so embarrassed that a girl—who was holding only his shirt with one hand—could not land a punch on her face— on my face. 9 The ending of that story is that we were called to the guidance but they didn’t call our parents. 10 That’s how my first dream, my first star, crashed.



Teenage Years

          1 I was determined to transform myself when I reached high school. 2 My goal at the time was to find a trustworthy circle of friends, become the top student academically, and create fun, unforgettable memories. 3 However, as 7th, 8th, 9th, and 10th grade passed, I remained the same Kathleen I had been in elementary school. 4 Yes, I found the circle of friends I had always wanted and got to experience some enjoyable moments, but nothing truly memorable. 5 I was still a quiet kid, unsure how to converse with anyone outside my friend group. 6 I desperately sought acceptance and attention by acting mysterious and reserved, but it never worked—and I realized it too late. 7 Then came 11th grade, during the pandemic, when the whole world was in quarantine. 8 During that time, I watched countless self-improvement videos on YouTube, trying different hacks, methods, and advice. 9 Some worked, while others didn’t. 10 In my mind, I believed I was becoming a better version of myself—one who could joke around, express emotions, and engage with others more freely . 11 When 12th grade arrived, our school finally allowed face-to-face classes again. 12 At first, I was excited to meet my classmates because I thought this new version of myself would make it easier to befriend people. 13 I believed that after all the self-improvement I had done, I would finally be more social, more confident—someone people would want to be around. 14 But I was wrong... again. 15 Not only did I struggle to befriend anyone, but I also felt like I was annoying. 16 Every time I tried to join a conversation, I overthought my words, afraid of saying something awkward. 17 I forced myself to be more outgoing, but instead of feeling natural, it felt like I was putting on an act—one that no one really noticed or cared about. 18 The worst part was realizing that despite all my efforts to change, I still felt like an outsider. 19 It was as if no matter how much I tried to reinvent myself, I was stuck in the same cycle of self-doubt and loneliness. 20 But looking back, I realize there were still some moments in my teenage years when I was truly myself—especially when I graduated from senior high school. 21 One of my best friends, Rona, was there. 22 We were able to graduate together since we attended the same school, She was someone I always turned to whenever I had problems, I would always message her to come along with me when I wanted to go shopping. 23 I was so thankful for her and all of my best friends at that time because, with them, there was no need to pretend, no need to wear a mask. 24 I could simply be myself. 25 Those moments reminded me that maybe, just maybe, I could be better—not by changing who I was, but by embracing it. 26 I guess you could say that this time, I was able to catch a star.



College Years

          1 As I stepped into college, I carried that hope with me—new environment, new faces, and a fresh start—this was my chance to finally grow into the person I had always wanted to be. 2 But little did I know, college would bring its own set of challenges and lessons that would shape me in ways I never expected. 3 When I was in my 1st yearof college, everything was going great—at least for the first half, since it was online. 4 I had gotten used to the comfort of studying at home, where I didn’t have to worry about social interactions or the pressure of making friends. 5 But when the university announced that face-to-face classes would resume in the second semester, I was not excited nor nervous. 6 I was normal, no reaction—just purely laziness saying, “ayoko pumasok ng f2f, nakakatamad.” This time, I didn’t bother setting any expectations. 8 I had learned my lesson in senior high—expectations only led to disappointment. 9 I didn’t want to hope for a fresh start or believe that I’d suddenly become more outgoing just because I was in a new environment. 10 Instead, I wanted to have a fresh start—not in the sense of becoming someone entirely new, but in truly discovering who I was. 11 I realized that finding myself wasn’t about chasing a single, fixed version of me—it was a continuous process, a journey of growth and change. 12 We are always evolving, shaped by our experiences, the people we meet, and the lessons we learn along the way. 13 But one thing I was certain of—I didn’t want to become someone I didn’t recognize. 14 I didn’t want to achieve my dreams only to realize that, in the process, I had lost myself. 15 Success means nothing if it comes at the cost of authenticity. 16 I’ve seen people who reached their goals yet felt empty because they weren’t truly happy with who they had become. 17 Especially in college, where you will meet people from different backgrounds and situations, it’s easy to lose yourself in the process of fitting in. 18 You see others chasing some things in ways you never considered, adopting mindsets and lifestyles that don’t align with who you are. 19 That’s why my goal in college is simple—and that is to just stay true to myself. 20 I don’t want to be swayed by others, to change just to blend in, or to adopt attitudes that don’t reflect who I really am. 21 But I’ll admit, staying true to yourself is easier said than done. 22 When I was in my 2nd year, I found myself getting influenced by the people around me. I tried to match their energy, their habits, their ways of thinking—It was a wake-up call. 24 I asked myself every time, why did I do that? And every time, I would know the answer—and it’s because deep down, I was actively chasing their approvals —wanting to be seen, to belong, to feel like I was enough. 26 I molded myself to fit in, thinking that if I acted a certain way, I would finally feel accepted. 27 But doing so, I realized that I was slowly drifting away from who I really was. 28 The worst part was that no matter how much I tried to align myself with them, I never truly felt like I belonged. 29 It was exhausting—I became so focused on being liked that I forgot to ask myself: are you truly happy? Still, to this day, knowing this and repeating the same cycle hurts. 31 But I want to get that star, I have hope, because at the end of the day, I don’t want to look back and realize I spent my college years pretending to be someone I’m not. 32 I want to graduate knowing that I stayed true to myself, made meaningful connections, and grew in a way that felt right for me—not for anyone else.



          1As I embark on my journey, I remind myself that the sun and stars will always exist. 2The sun guides me through moments of clarity, while the stars remain steady, like my dreams, even in darkness. 3At times, I may feel lost or doubt myself, but my dreams never fade—they evolve and grow with me. 4I may not have everything figured out, but I know I must pursue my dreams by embracing who I am. 5My path isn’t about proving myself to others but finding fulfillment in my own way. 6As long as I keep moving forward, I believe I will one day catch my own light.
